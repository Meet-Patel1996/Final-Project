Healthy Foods that Lift Your Mood


When you're feeling down, it's easy to turn to food to make you feel better. However, the sugary, high-calorie treats to which many people turn have their own set of negative consequences. As a result, you might be wondering if there are any safe foods that can help you feel better. Nonetheless, studies have shown that certain foods can help with general brain health and certain forms of mood disorders. Here are 5 balanced foods that will boost your mood.


1. Dark chocolate


Cocoa has the highest flavonoid content of any food by weight, which is why cocoa-based items like chocolate contribute greatly to dietary flavonoid intake. Consumption of flavonoid-rich cocoa products has been shown to improve brain health.


2. Berries


Surprisingly, consuming more fruits and vegetables has been related to lower depression rates. Berries are high in a number of compounds that can help you do better in school and protect your brain's health. Blueberries, strawberries, and blackberries, for example, are particularly rich in flavonoid compounds called anthocyanins.


3. Fermented Foods


Kimchi, yogurt, kefir, kombucha, and sauerkraut are examples of fermented foods that may improve gut health and mood. Fermentation enables live bacteria to flourish in foods, allowing them to turn sugars into alcohol and acids. Probiotics are produced during this process. These live microorganisms help healthy bacteria develop in your gut and can raise serotonin levels.


4. Nuts


Nuts are high in vitamins E and zinc, both of which are essential for brain health. They're also convenient and adaptable, making them ideal for study snacks.


5. Oats


Oats are a whole grain that can boost the mood during the day. They come in a variety of kinds, including overnight oats, oatmeal, muesli, and granola. They're a great source of fiber, with 8 grams in just one raw cup. Fiber slows carbohydrate absorption, allowing for a gradual release of sugar into the bloodstream and maintaining energy levels.
